ICC Strategy (Indication ‚Üí Correction ‚Üí Continuation)

üîç Indication

What it is: The initial move that establishes trend bias. This is the first leg that shows directional strength.

What to look for:

  • Strong directional candles (large body, small wicks)
  • Volume expansion on the move
  • Breaking through a key level or liquidity pool
  • Occurs during high-volume sessions (NY open, London/NY overlap)

Key: This sets your bias. If you miss the Indication, wait for the next setup‚Äîdon't chase.

‚Ü©Ô∏è Correction

What it is: The pullback or retracement after the Indication. Price returns to test structure or liquidity.

What to look for:

  • Shallow retracement (30-50% of Indication move ideal)
  • Smaller candles, choppy action = weak sellers/buyers
  • Pulls back to key support/resistance, VWAP, or prior swing
  • Time spent correcting (not too fast, not too long)

Key: This is your entry zone. Mark your levels during the Correction phase.

üöÄ Continuation

What it is: Price resumes in the direction of the Indication. This is where you enter and ride the move.

What to look for:

  • Strong candle in Indication direction breaking Correction high/low
  • Volume returns (confirms buyers/sellers are back)
  • Clean rejection of your entry level
  • Enter on break of Correction structure, stop below/above entry level

Key: Enter as Continuation starts. Target prior high/low or next liquidity zone.

‚úÖ A+ ICC Setup Checklist:

  • Clear Indication with volume during NY session (9:30-11am ET ideal)
  • Correction holds a key level (prior swing, VWAP, overnight range)
  • Continuation candle shows strength and breaks Correction structure
  • Risk is defined: stop just beyond Correction extreme
  • Reward is clear: prior high/low or next liquidity pool at 1.5-2R minimum

Position Sizing Examples

Position sizing protects your capital and keeps you in the game. Risk a fixed % per trade (0.5-1%) so no single trade can hurt you.

Example 1: MES Trade ($10,000 account, 1% risk)

  • Account: $10,000
  • Risk per trade: 1% = $100
  • Entry: 4500.00, Stop: 4496.00 (4 points = 16 ticks)
  • Risk in ticks: 16 ticks
  • MES tick value: $1.25/tick
  • Risk per contract: 16 ticks √ó $1.25 = $20
  • Contracts: $100 √∑ $20 = 5 contracts

If stopped out, you lose $100 (1%). If target at 4506.00 (6 points), you gain 24 ticks √ó $1.25 √ó 5 = $150 (1.5R).

Example 2: MNQ Trade ($5,000 account, 0.5% risk)

  • Account: $5,000
  • Risk per trade: 0.5% = $25
  • Entry: 16800.00, Stop: 16790.00 (10 points = 40 ticks)
  • Risk in ticks: 40 ticks
  • MNQ tick value: $0.50/tick
  • Risk per contract: 40 ticks √ó $0.50 = $20
  • Contracts: $25 √∑ $20 = 1 contract

If stopped out, you lose $20. If target at 16820.00 (20 points), you gain 80 ticks √ó $0.50 √ó 1 = $40 (2R).

üìê Formula: Contracts = (Account √ó Risk%) √∑ (Stop Distance in Ticks √ó Tick Value)

Always round DOWN. Never risk more than your rule allows. Start conservative (0.5%) until consistent.

Market Session Timing

üïê Session Overview

  • Asia Session: 6pm-3am ET ‚Äî Slow, range-bound. Sets overnight high/low.
  • London Open: 3am-4am ET ‚Äî First volatility spike. Watch for liquidity sweeps.
  • NY Open: 9:30am-11:00am ET ‚Äî PRIME TIME. Highest volume, cleanest trends.
  • Lunch: 11:30am-1:30pm ET ‚Äî Chop, low volume. Avoid unless strong trend continues.
  • NY Close: 3pm-4pm ET ‚Äî Final push or reversal. Can be volatile but tricky.

‚è∞ Best Times to Trade ICC

  • 9:30-11:00am ET: Indication forms at open, Correction during first pullback, Continuation by 10-11am.
  • 8:30-9:30am ET: Pre-market can show Indication if economic data releases. Wait for NY open confirmation.
  • Avoid: Asian session (too slow), lunch hour (choppy), last 15 min of day (unpredictable).

üìÖ Days to Trade

  • Tuesday-Thursday: Most consistent. Market is "warmed up" and not distorted by weekend gaps.
  • Monday: Can be choppy as market digests weekend news. Wait for clear structure.
  • Friday: Watch for early profit-taking and position squaring. Trends can fade after 11am.

Reading Charts & Price Action

üìä Timeframe Strategy

Higher Timeframe (1h-4h): Identifies overall bias and major structure.

  • Mark major swing highs/lows, support/resistance zones
  • Identify trend: Are we making higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows?
  • Locate liquidity pools (above highs, below lows)

Mid Timeframe (15m): Refines structure and shows ICC pattern clearly.

  • Spot the Indication leg (strong directional move)
  • Track the Correction (pullback to key level)
  • Identify entry zones for Continuation

Lower Timeframe (1m-5m): Times your entry precisely.

  • Wait for Continuation candle to break Correction structure
  • Enter on strong candle close in trend direction
  • Set stop just beyond Correction extreme (2-4 points for micros)

üîç What to Look For

Strong Candles (Indication/Continuation):

  • Large body, small wicks = conviction
  • Closes near high (uptrend) or low (downtrend)
  • Multiple candles in same direction = momentum

Weak Candles (Correction):

  • Small bodies, large wicks = indecision
  • Overlapping candles = chop, no follow-through
  • Low volume = pause, not reversal

Key Levels:

  • Prior day high/low
  • Overnight high/low
  • Opening range (first 30 min of NY session)
  • VWAP (volume-weighted average price)
  • Round numbers (4500, 16800, etc.)

Remember: Price respects levels where volume traded. Breaks without volume usually fail.

Entry & Exit Techniques

üéØ Entry Rules

When to Enter:

  • Wait for Continuation candle to close beyond Correction high/low
  • Enter immediately after strong candle close OR on pullback to breakout level
  • Confirm: Is trend still valid on higher timeframe? Is volume present?
  • Set stop 2-4 points beyond Correction extreme (just outside structure)

Entry Types:

  • Aggressive: Enter on break of Correction structure (less confirmation, tighter risk)
  • Conservative: Wait for retest of breakout level (more confirmation, may miss move)
  • Partial: Enter half position on break, half on retest

üí° Tip: If you hesitate on entry, the setup probably isn't A+. Skip it and wait for the next one.

üö™ Exit Rules

Stop Loss (Always Set First):

  • Place 2-4 points beyond Correction extreme
  • NEVER move stop against your position (no "giving it room")
  • If stopped out, setup was invalid‚Äîmove on

Profit Target:

  • First target: Prior swing high/low from Indication (1.5-2R minimum)
  • Extended target: Next liquidity pool or round number (if trend is very strong)
  • Partial exit: Take half at 1.5R, let rest run to 2-3R with trailing stop

Exit Signals:

  • Target hit = close full position
  • Momentum slows (small candles, no follow-through) = exit at break-even or small profit
  • Reversal candle against you on 5m = exit immediately
  • Time-based: If no progress after 15-20 min, consider exiting

‚ö†Ô∏è Golden Rule: Protect Your Capital First

Never risk more than 1% per trade. Never move stops against you. Never add to a losing position. A bad exit you can survive. A bad entry without a stop will end you.

Candles vs Indicators

Candles (OHLC)
Candles show open, high, low, close and wicks ‚Äî the raw facts of buyer/seller behavior.
Indicator (Moving Average)
Indicators summarize past price (they lag). ICC relies on candles for structure, rejection, and timing.
